Experimental Debriefing

 

Thank you for your participation in our experiment. This document serves as short description of the purposes behind our experiment.

 

You just completed what is commonly known as a flanker task. On each trial you were presented with a row of letters. Your task was to identify the center letter and ignore the letters on the side. Sometimes the letter on the side matches the target center letter, and sometimes it does not.

 

Typically, response times to identify the target letter are faster for matching than mismatching conditions. This difference in response time provides a measure of attention, or your ability to focus exclusively on the target letter and ignore the other letters. We conducting this study to investigate these spatial aspects of human attention across a wide range of participants.

 

Thank you again for your participation.
